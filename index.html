Consulting Recruiting Snapshot

Start earlier than you think: consulting recruiting rewards a prepared resume, targeted networking, and live case practice. Management Consulted's 2026 guidance frames early-career consulting deadlines as concentrated between March and October, notes that firms often hire far in advance, and recommends networking before applying. For interviews, students should expect fit plus case rounds, practice out loud, build mental-math and structuring habits, and work toward a substantial live-case practice volume before interviews.

Consulting Recruiting Timeline

Use the timeline as a planning tool: first learn the consulting types and entry titles, then prepare a consulting resume, LinkedIn profile, and story bank. Begin beginner case practice and mental-math work before applications open, schedule alumni conversations early, then track deadlines across 12Twenty, LinkedIn, firm sites, and the Kogod Management Consulted resource. Do not assume every consulting path follows the same cycle: strategy, federal/public sector, technology, and human capital consulting can move on different timelines.

Case Prep Expectations
Expect case interviews to test structure, business judgment, mental math, and communication out loud. Build toward 25-50 live practice cases, keep a mistake log, and practice fit stories alongside cases. Prioritize profitability, market entry, pricing, M&A, and growth cases; practice giving a crisp recommendation under time pressure.
Networking Expectations
Start networking before applications open. Aim for short, specific conversations with alumni or consultants, then follow up with a focused note about your function interest and what you learned. For strategy roles, ask how the firm staffs generalist vs industry-focused work and what strong analyst/associate performance looks like in the first year.
Timeline by Consulting Type
Strategy consulting often has the most compressed and early recruiting cycle. Students should begin case prep, resume polishing, and alumni outreach before postings appear, then track firm-specific deadlines closely.
